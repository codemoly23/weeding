# LLC Formation Policies & Requirements (2025)

This document contains all the policies, rules, and requirements for LLC formation services. Use this as a reference for FAQ creation.

---

## 1. LLC Types

### Single-Member LLC
- **Definition**: An LLC with only one owner (member)
- **Ownership**: 100% owned by single member
- **Taxation**: By default, treated as "disregarded entity" for tax purposes (reported on personal tax return)
- **Simplicity**: Simplest structure, less paperwork
- **Best for**: Solo entrepreneurs, freelancers, small business owners

### Multi-Member LLC
- **Definition**: An LLC with two or more owners (members)
- **Ownership**: Divided among members by percentage (must total 100%)
- **Taxation**: By default, treated as partnership for tax purposes
- **Requirements**:
  - All members must be listed with their ownership percentages
  - Total ownership must equal exactly 100%
  - Each member needs: Full name, email, contact information
- **Operating Agreement**: Strongly recommended (often required by banks)
- **Best for**: Business partners, family businesses, joint ventures

## 2. Management Structures

### Member-Managed LLC (Default)
- **Definition**: All members actively participate in managing the LLC's day-to-day operations
- **Decision Making**: All members have authority to make business decisions
- **Best for**:
  - Smaller LLCs where all owners are actively involved
  - Businesses where owners want direct control
- **Most Common**: This is the default and most popular choice

### Manager-Managed LLC
- **Definition**: One or more designated managers handle day-to-day operations
- **Manager Options**:
  1. **Member-Manager**: One of the LLC members serves as the designated manager
  2. **Non-Member Manager**: Someone hired from outside the LLC to manage operations
- **Members' Role**: Members are passive investors; they don't participate in daily decisions
- **Best for**:
  - Larger LLCs with many members
  - LLCs with passive investors
  - When professional management is needed
- **Non-Member Manager Requirements**:
  - Full legal name
  - Email address
  - Phone number
  - Physical address
  - City and country

## 3. Profit & Loss Distribution

### Proportional Distribution (Default)
- Profits and losses are distributed based on each member's ownership percentage
- Example: If Member A owns 60% and Member B owns 40%, profits split 60/40
- **Most common and straightforward method**

### Equal Distribution
- All members receive equal shares regardless of ownership percentage
- Example: 3 members = each gets 33.33% of profits
- Must be explicitly stated in Operating Agreement

### Custom Distribution
- Members agree on a specific distribution formula
- Can be based on: work contribution, capital contribution, or any agreed formula
- Must be detailed in Operating Agreement
- Requires careful documentation

### Basic LLC Information
| Field | Required | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| LLC Name | Yes | Must contain "LLC", "L.L.C.", or "Limited Liability Company" |
| Alternative Names | Optional | Up to 2 backup names if first choice unavailable |
| Formation State | Yes | State where LLC will be registered |

| Business Industry | Yes | Description of primary business activity |

## 5. State-Specific Information

### Wyoming (Recommended)
- **State Filing Fee**: $100
- **Annual Report Fee**: $60/year (minimum)
- **Privacy**: No requirement to list members/managers publicly
- **Processing Time**: 10-15 business days (standard), 1-2 days (expedited)
- **Benefits**:
  - No state income tax
  - Strong asset protection laws
  - Low annual fees
  - High privacy protection
  - Favorable business laws

### Delaware
- **State Filing Fee**: $90
- **Annual Franchise Tax**: $300/year
- **Privacy**: Good privacy protection
- **Benefits**:
  - Well-established business law (Court of Chancery)
  - Preferred by investors and VCs
  - Flexible operating agreement rules

### Nevada
- **State Filing Fee**: $425 (includes Initial List)
- **Annual Report Fee**: $150/year
- **Benefits**:
  - No state corporate income tax
  - No franchise tax
  - Strong privacy laws

### New Mexico
- **State Filing Fee**: $50
- **Annual Report**: None required
- **Benefits**:
  - Lowest filing fee
  - No annual reports
  - Good for privacy

## 6. Additional Services

### EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- **What**: Federal tax ID number from IRS
- **Required For**:
  - Opening business bank account
  - Hiring employees
  - Filing business taxes
- **Processing**: Usually 1-2 business days
- **Cost**: Free from IRS, service fee applies for assistance

### Registered Agent
- **What**: Person or company that receives legal documents on behalf of LLC
- **Requirements**:
  - Must have physical address in formation state
  - Must be available during business hours
- **Why Needed**:
  - Required by all states
  - Maintains privacy (agent's address is public, not owner's)
  - Ensures legal documents are received promptly

### Virtual US Address
- **What**: Physical US address for receiving mail
- **Benefits**:
  - Provides US presence for international owners
  - Can be used for business correspondence
  - Mail scanning and forwarding available

### Banking Assistance
- **What**: Help opening US business bank account
- **Challenges for International Owners**:
  - Many US banks require in-person visit
  - Some banks don't accept foreign-owned LLCs
- **Solution**: Partner banks that accept remote applications

## 7. BOI (Beneficial Ownership Information) Reporting - 2025 Update

### Major Change (March 2025)
**Domestic US LLCs are now EXEMPT from BOI reporting requirements.**

### Current Rules
- Only **foreign companies** registered to do business in the US must file BOI reports
- US persons are exempt from providing BOI information
- Domestic LLCs (including those owned by foreigners) do NOT need to file BOI reports

### Source
FinCEN Interim Final Rule, March 26, 2025
